Kit_1.0 HomeLab Story and Requirements.

Powerful & Low Cost!!! The above parts list is my best take at assembling a homelab for around 1k. The goal for this list is to make a basic entry level homelab kit that is a good starting point to enter the space.

The design uses 2u of the 12u rack space leaving you 10u of space to add in servers, switches, firewalls, power sources, and anything else that you might want to add to improve your network. (You will have room to grow!)

The Switch is unmanaged but that's ok you will have many other things to work on when setting up your server for the first time so a plug and play switch works nice as a beginner. You might want to look for a 24 port solutions if your looking to scale your network in the future.

Pros: Low cost, Room to Scale Up, Powerful server

Cons: Loud when running at full power, Minimal gb storage space, Switch could be upgraded

This Kit is a good example of all the items you will need when building your home cloud. If it were ice cream it would be vanilla, nothing to exciting but it will work great.

A quick note: You will need a monitor with a vga port and usb keyboard to set up the server when first getting started. If you don't have them lying around the house you will have to purchase theses items or borrow them from a friend).

This Kit_1.0 is for people like me that want to get started in homelabing on a tight budget.
